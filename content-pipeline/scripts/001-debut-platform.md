### Script (English, ~7 min)

**0:00-0:30 — Hook**
> 6 AI agents. One 24/7 content team. Trend scouting, topic selection, scriptwriting, platform ops, monetization — fully automated. My only job? Approve.
>
> Hi, I'm [name], an AI Product Manager in cross-border e-commerce. This is not a concept video. This is the system I use every single day to produce content without burning out.

**0:30-1:15 — The Real Problem**
> Sound familiar? Spot a trend → 2 hours picking a topic → 4 hours scripting → 6 hours filming and editing → hit publish → 200 views. So you learn another AI tool. Rinse and repeat.
>
> I was in that loop too. Until I realized: content creation isn't a creativity problem. It's a systems engineering problem. The tools aren't the bottleneck — the missing system is.

**1:15-2:30 — DOMA Framework**
> Step one: define your business ontology.
>
> I use the DOMA decision intelligence architecture. D is Data — trend data, engagement data, user behavior. O is Ontology — the structured representation of your business logic: creator positioning, audience profiles, content taxonomy. This is the most critical layer. As the founder of 中科闻歌 said: "Without ontology, the LLM doesn't understand your business."
>
> M is Models — Claude for reasoning, Kimi for long-form analysis. A is Agents — the execution layer that turns analysis into action.
>
> The key insight: don't just plug in a model and hope it works. Define your ontology first, then let models and agents operate within structured rules.

**2:30-3:45 — Hybrid Topology**
> For the Agents layer, I chose a Hybrid topology — 6 specialized agents coordinating dynamically:
>
> 1. Trend Scout — 24/7 cross-platform trend scraping
> 2. User Researcher — continuous audience modeling
> 3. Video Architect — the agent talking to you right now
> 4. Knowledge Curator — manages the knowledge base, connects all context
> 5. Platform Engineer — distribution, publishing, analytics
> 6. Monetization Strategist — finds revenue opportunities
>
> They don't pass work in a straight line. They communicate, negotiate, and coordinate — a network, not a pipeline.

**3:45-5:00 — TDD-R**
> How does the system get better over time? TDD-R loop.
>
> Train: feed historical data and domain knowledge to each agent.
>
> Diagnose: after every video, automatically collect retention, engagement data. Real story: my retention dropped sharply at 45 seconds. The Diagnose phase identified it was information overload — too many concepts without breathers.
>
> Deploy: the system automatically updated the script module's parameters — now it inserts a quotable line or data point every 30 seconds. No human intervention.
>
> Reflect: weekly system-wide review, extract patterns, optimize the design.
>
> This is Dogfooding at its best: you build it, you use it. The fastest feedback loop a PM can ask for.

**5:00-6:00 — A/B-Ready Planning**
> Every piece of content ships with pre-baked test variables:
>
> Two hook versions — digital shock vs. controversy. Three headline variants — pain-point, curiosity, benefit. Two cover directions — data-driven big text vs. personal emotion.
>
> Long-form gets auto-clipped into a 60-second short for TikTok/Reels.
>
> The goal isn't to run experiments right now. The goal is to make sure every video has comparability genes. When you have traffic volume, you can run retrospective A/B tests without recreating anything.

**6:00-6:30 — CTA**
